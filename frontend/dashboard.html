Quais os extratos de plantas que possuem maior potencial fotoprotetor in vitro e in vivo?

Os maiores potenciais fotoprotetores foram observados nos extratos de Lippia microphylla (SPF 26,82) e Dimorphandra gardneriana (SPF 20,12) in vitro, e em extratos de plantas antárticas in vivo

Abstract

This review of ten studies compared the photoprotective potential of plant extracts evaluated in vitro—and in one case, in vivo. Twenty-one plant species were assessed with extraction methods that were mostly methanolic or ethanolic. In vitro assessments using SPF determination and antioxidant assays indicate that several extracts show promise. For example:

  1. Lippia microphylla extract achieved an SPF of 26.82
  2. Dimorphandra gardneriana extract reported an SPF of 20.12.
  3. Euphorbia tirucalli extract yielded SPF values ranging from 6.05 to 19.84

In vivo, Antarctic plant extracts reduced UVC-induced DNA damage and lipid peroxidation in cell and animal models, suggesting that their photoprotection may extend to biological systems. Several studies also document a close correlation between antioxidant capacity and SPF values, suggesting that the mechanism of photoprotection may be linked to the ability to scavenge free radicals.
